## How to Verify Kratom Quality Before You Buy

Quality verification isn't complicated. It just requires knowing what to check.

**Step 1: Look for a Certificate of Analysis (COA).** This is a document from an independent lab showing test results. It should list the specific batch number, test date, and results for alkaloid content, heavy metals, and microbial contaminants.

**Step 2: Confirm the lab is independent.** The lab should be a third party, not the vendor's own facility. Look for ISO-accredited labs.

**Step 3: Check the alkaloid profile.** Quality kratom powder typically contains 1-1.8% mitragynine by weight. Products significantly outside this range may be adulterated or degraded.

**Step 4: Evaluate the physical product.** Good kratom powder should be finely ground, have a consistent color (varies by strain), and smell fresh and earthy. Clumpy, stale-smelling, or off-color powder suggests poor handling or age.

**Step 5: Start with a small order.** Before committing to bulk purchases, order a small amount (1-2 ounces) to evaluate quality firsthand.

Here's the difference between a tested and untested product: with tested kratom, you know the alkaloid content, so you can dose accurately (most people start with 2-4 grams). With untested kratom, you're guessing, and that affects both effectiveness and safety.

## Common Mistakes When Buying Kratom

**Buying based on price alone.** The cheapest kratom is almost never the best. Low prices often mean skipped testing, poor sourcing, or old inventory.

**Ignoring lab results.** If a vendor doesn't provide them, move on. This is non-negotiable for safety.

**Buying from random marketplace sellers.** Third-party marketplace platforms sometimes have kratom listings from unverified sellers. There's no quality control. Avoid these.

**Not checking the legal status.** Ordering kratom to a state or city where it's banned can result in confiscated packages and potential legal issues.

**Expecting identical effects from every batch.** Kratom is a natural plant product. Alkaloid levels vary between batches, even from the same vendor. Good vendors minimize this variation through testing and blending, but some difference is normal.

**Storing kratom improperly.** Keep powder in airtight containers away from light, heat, and moisture. Properly stored kratom maintains potency for 3-6 months. Poorly stored kratom degrades faster.
